So, I woke up one day to an alarming realization: I am a white male in his
30s with neither a podcast nor a substack to his name.

The thing is, I fucking love writing. If you know me from work, you've probably
noticed this. But the tricky bit has always been finding something I enjoy
writing about. One day, after a particularly satisfying root-cause analysis
report, I asked Claude for ideas on how to scratch that itch under the following
restrictions:

- I have nothing important to say
- I write for my own entertainment, but journaling feels too hollow
- I don't like chasing strangers to follow me

And after some back and forth, it pitched this idea: a technically-public
collection of thoughts, with zero interactive elements, no engagement metrics,
and no specific purpose.

You know when you get a song stuck in your head, and the best way to get it out
is to listen to the whole song, all the way to the end? My mind feels like that
all the time, only the songs have no end. This is where I write them.

Apparently this is going to be a, *(checks first 5 articles in the queue)*,
literary criticism blog. It's not supposed to be. Mostly, I plan to write about
the things on my mind. Right now, that happens to be mostly children's stories,
but in due time, I imagine there will be a lot of diversity here.

These are all conversation starters, but the conversations will happen
elsewhere.
